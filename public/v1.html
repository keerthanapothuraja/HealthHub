VISUAL IMPAIREMENT

1. National Programme for Control of Blindness and Visual Impairment (NPCB&VI):

This scheme aims to reduce the prevalence of blindness and visual impairment by providing comprehensive eye care services, including cataract surgeries, distribution of spectacles, treatment for eye diseases, and rehabilitation services.

2. Assistance to Disabled Persons for Purchase/Fitting of Aids and Appliances (ADIP Scheme):

Under this scheme, visually impaired individuals can receive aids and appliances such as braille kits, talking calculators, screen readers, and other assistive devices to enhance their independence and quality of life.

3. Accessible India Campaign (Sugamya Bharat Abhiyan):

While it aims to improve accessibility for individuals with various disabilities, including mobility and hearing impairments, this campaign also focuses on making public infrastructure, information, and communication technology (ICT) accessible for people with visual impairments. This includes provisions such as installing tactile paths, braille signage, and accessible websites and mobile applications.

4. National Scholarship for Persons with Disabilities (NSPwD):

This scholarship scheme provides financial assistance to students with disabilities, including those with visual impairments, to pursue higher education. It covers various educational expenses, including tuition fees, books, and other related costs.

5. Rashtriya Vayoshri Yojana:

This scheme focuses on providing assistive devices to senior citizens belonging to the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category, including those with visual impairments. It includes items such as spectacles, walking sticks, hearing aids, and other aids and appliances to enhance their visual capabilities and overall quality of life.
